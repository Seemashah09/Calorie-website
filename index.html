How Does the Calorie Calculator Work?

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which is considered the most accurate formula for estimating daily caloric needs. It accounts for your age, gender, weight, height, and activity level.

For weight loss, we recommend a 500 calorie deficit (about 0.5 kg/week), and for weight gain, a 500 calorie surplus. Always consult a healthcare professional before making significant dietary changes.

What Is BMR?

Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body needs to perform basic life-sustaining functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production. It typically accounts for 60-75% of your total daily energy expenditure.
